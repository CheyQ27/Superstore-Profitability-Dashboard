📘 Overview

This project analyzes sales and profitability data from a retail superstore to identify key drivers of profit and loss across different regions, product categories, and customer segments. Using SQL for data exploration and Tableau for visualization, the analysis uncovers trends and patterns that can inform better business decisions and improve overall profitability.

🧠 Objectives
Identify top-performing and underperforming products and categories
Analyze profit trends across regions, segments, and time
Detect patterns in sales that negatively impact profitability
Provide data-driven recommendations to improve business performance
🗄️ Dataset

File: Superstore dataset (Tableau workbook)
Description: Contains transactional retail data including sales, profit, product categories, customer segments, and geographic information.

Key Fields:

Order Date
Region
Category & Sub-Category
Sales
Profit
Customer Segment

🛠️ Tools
PostgreSQL (for data querying and analysis)
SQL (data cleaning, aggregation, and trend analysis)
Tableau (interactive dashboard and data visualization)

📝 Key Queries
Aggregating total sales and profit by category and region
Identifying top and bottom performing sub-categories
Calculating profit margins across segments
Analyzing time-based trends (monthly/quarterly performance)
Detecting loss-making products using conditional filtering

📈 Insights
Certain sub-categories consistently generate losses despite high sales volume, indicating pricing or cost inefficiencies
Profitability varies significantly by region, with some regions outperforming others due to product mix and demand
High sales do not always correlate with high profit, highlighting the importance of margin analysis
Specific customer segments contribute more to profitability, suggesting targeted marketing opportunities
